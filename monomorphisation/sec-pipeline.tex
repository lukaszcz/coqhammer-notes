\section{The composed pipeline}
\label{sec:pipeline}

We assemble the stages and state the end-to-end guarantee.

\begin{definition}[Pipeline]
\label{def:pipeline}
Fix an admissible environment, premises $P$ (valid closed
propositions) and a closed goal $\varphi_G$.  Fix parameters $K,
\Delta_{\mathrm{tot}}, \Delta_c$ (defaults $3, 200, 16$).  The
\emph{monomorphising pipeline} computes:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(P\arabic*),leftmargin=3em]
\item \emph{Saturate:} a monomorphisation $\Theta$ by the bounded
  saturation of \cref{def:saturation}, including instances of the
  implicit inductive premises (\cref{def:mono-op});
\item \emph{Instantiate:} $P' \coloneqq \Mono_\Theta(P)$, with
  policy \emph{drop} (default for the stages below) or \emph{keep};
\item \emph{Translate:} $(\Phi_0, \gamma) \coloneqq
  \mathrm{Transl}(P', \varphi_G)$ (\cref{def:transl-problem});
\item \emph{Prune and specialise} (drop policy, first-order core
  only): $(\Phi_1, \gamma_1) \coloneqq \spec{}(\Phi_0', \gamma)$
  where $\Phi_0'$ prunes as in \cref{lem:spec-total};
\item \emph{Erase:} compute the erasable sorts
  (\cref{def:erasable}: witnessed, and NV-safe or
  inductive-infinite --- both syntactic checks), choose $\Sigma_0$
  among them, and output $(\Phi_2, \gamma_2) \coloneqq
  (\erase{\Sigma_0}(\Phi_1), \erase{\Sigma_0}(\gamma_1))$.
\end{enumerate}
The problem $(\Phi_2, \gamma_2)$ is handed to the ATPs.  If a
refutation is found, the premises used in it are mapped back
through the instance bookkeeping ($\inst{\varphi_p}{\theta} \mapsto
p$) and given to the certified reconstruction backend.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[End-to-end soundness]
\label{thm:pipeline}
Suppose the hypotheses of
\cref{lem:spec-total,lem:pipeline-sorted} hold for the pipeline run
(first-order core, witnessed quantified types) and $\Sigma_0$
consists of erasable sorts.  If $\Phi_2 \folproves \gamma_2$, then
$\varphi_G$ is valid in $\MM$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \cref{thm:mono-sound}, $(\Phi_0, \gamma)$ is $\MM$-sound;
pruning removes axioms, preserving $\MM$-soundness trivially; by
\cref{thm:spec-sound}, $(\Phi_1, \gamma_1)$ is $\MM$-sound.  By
\cref{lem:pipeline-sorted}, $\Phi_1 \cup
\{\mathrm{nnf}(\neg\gamma_1)\}$ is a sorted core, so by
\cref{thm:erasure-main} it is $\Sigma_0$-inflatable, and
\cref{thm:erasure-from-inflatable} converts the assumed provability
into validity of $\varphi_G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Relative completeness of the back end]
\label{prop:pipeline-complete}
Stages (P4)--(P5) lose no proofs: if $\Phi_0' \folproves \gamma$
then $\Phi_2 \folproves \gamma_2$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\Cref{thm:spec-faithful} (totality by \cref{lem:spec-total}) and
\cref{prop:erasure-complete}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Where incompleteness is accepted]
Stage (P2) with the drop policy is incomplete by design
(\cref{prop:drop-incomplete}), and provably cannot be repaired by a
cleverer computable instance selection
(\cref{thm:undecidable}); the keep policy restores completeness
(\cref{prop:mono-conservative}) at the price of retaining the
generic formulas, for which the erasure theory of
\cref{sec:erasure} is not available
(\cref{rem:erasure-scope}(iii)).  The pruning inside (P4) removes
the application-typing axioms; for constant-headed terms their
content is subsumed by the flattened typing axioms, so we expect no
practical loss, but we make no formal claim.  A hammer can, and
should, hedge: CoqHammer already runs several ATPs in parallel with
different translation options \cite{CzajkaKaliszyk2018}, and the
natural deployment runs the drop-policy pipeline with erasure
alongside a keep-policy or unmonomorphised run.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Absolute safety]
\label{rem:absolute-safety}
Independently of every theorem in this paper, the hammer
architecture is fail-safe: the ATP output is used only to select
premises, and the goal is re-proved from the selected premises by
the certified search tactics and checked by the proof-assistant
kernel \cite{CzajkaKaliszyk2018}.  What
\cref{thm:pipeline} adds is the guarantee that the optimised
pipeline does not manufacture refutations of consistent problems
--- i.e.\ that ATP ``successes'' remain correlated with true goals,
which is what makes the tool useful rather than merely safe.  The
necessity examples
(\cref{prop:nec-inhab,prop:nec-mono}) show this guarantee is not
academic: unrestricted guard erasure produces exactly such phantom
refutations.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Implementation notes]
\label{rem:implementation}
In CoqHammer's architecture the pipeline slots in as follows.
Stage (P1)--(P2) operates on the $\mathrm{CIC}_0$ representation
after premise selection and before the translation proper (in
\texttt{coq\_transl.ml} terms: between reinitialisation of the
definition table and retranslation), with instances entered as new
definitions with content-derived names so that per-name axiom
caching continues to work; the instance-to-premise name map rides
along for reconstruction.  Stage (P4) generalises the existing
\texttt{tconst}-specialisation and arity optimisation of
\texttt{tptp\_out.ml} \cite[\S5.5]{CzajkaKaliszyk2018}, with the
bridging axioms removed as analysed in \cref{sec:specialization};
stage (P5) is new, and its erasability check needs only the
inductive declarations and the instance set.  Suggested options:
\texttt{Set Hammer Monomorphisation} (policy and bounds),
\texttt{Set Hammer GuardSpecialization}, \texttt{Set Hammer
GuardErasure}.  Evaluation should follow the ablation grid
baseline / +specialisation / +monomorphisation /
+monomorphisation+specialisation / +all, on the standard
\texttt{eval/} benchmark, measuring ATP-provability and
reconstruction rates per prover.
\end{remark}
