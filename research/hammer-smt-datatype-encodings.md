# Research report: encodings of inductive types (esp. indexed families) in translations to FOL/SMT

*Produced by a web-research agent, 2026-07-16, for `notes/indexed_families.md`.
Primary PDFs archived in `../papers/` where available.*

**Placement of CoqHammer's own work.** Czajka & Kaliszyk (JAR 2018) and Czajka's
shallow sound PTS embedding define the current CoqHammer translation: untyped
FOL, per-occurrence shallow expansion of type membership into guards, no global
datatype axiomatization. Everything below is the comparative landscape around
that design point. Notably, Aguirre's F\* report (§4/§6 below) explicitly cites
Czajka's PTS-to-minimal-FOL soundness proof as the closest prior soundness
result, and observes that hammer-style *monotonicity-based guard removal*
"cannot apply" to F\* because "inductive types in F\* are not monotonous" — a
remark directly relevant to any plan to erase guards on indexed families.

---

## 1. Isabelle/Sledgehammer — ATP side (Meng & Paulson, JAR 2008) and the type-encoding line

**Citations.** Meng & Paulson, *Translating Higher-Order Clauses to First-Order
Clauses*, JAR 40:35–60, 2008. Blanchette, Böhme, Popescu, Smallbone, *Encoding
Monomorphic and Polymorphic Types*, LMCS 12(4), 2016 (arXiv:1609.08916).

**What is emitted for datatypes: nothing special.** Sledgehammer's ATP
translation emits **no datatype axioms of its own**. Datatype facts
(injectivity, distinctness, exhaustiveness/nchotomy, case equations, induction
instances) are ordinary Isabelle theorems generated by the datatype package and
passed through the relevance filter like any lemma. The translation machinery
is entirely about types and λs: application operator `@`, predicate `B(x)`
("x = True") coercing boolean terms to formulas, `fequal` with axiom
`∀X Y. fequal X Y ⟺ X = Y`; λ-removal by combinators or λ-lifting;
extensionality clause. Types become FOL *terms*; three encodings: fully-typed
(every subterm wrapped `ti(t, τ)`), partially-typed, constant-typed (compact,
unsound).

**The canonical exhaustiveness pitfall (§2.3, verbatim example).** For
`datatype two = a | b`, the theorem `∀x::two. x = a ∨ x = b`, translated
*untyped*, becomes `X=a | X=b`, which "asserts that the universe consists of
two elements; given a few axioms about the natural numbers or lists, ATPs
easily detect the inconsistency." Pragmatic mitigations (§2.8): rely on
Isabelle-side reconstruction to reject unsound proofs, and *filter out theorems
expressing finiteness* (anything mentioning `unit`, `bool`-typed variables,
induction rules). Faced in 2008, solved only heuristically.

**The principled fix (LMCS 2016).** Full type erasure is complete but
**unsound**: "equality can be used to encode cardinality constraints on
domains. For example, the axiom `∀U:unit. U ≈ unity` forces the domain of
*unit* to have only one element." Meng & Paulson's finiteness filter "makes the
translation incomplete and generally does not suffice to prevent unsound
cardinality reasoning." Sound encodings: type arguments `a` (unsound alone for
cardinalities), type tags `t` (every term wrapped — sound, complete), type
guards `g` (relativization predicates + nonemptiness axioms `∃X. g(σ,X)`), and
**monotonicity-based lightweight variants** (Claessen et al.): a type is
*monotonic* if any model can be extended with more elements of it; tags/guards
of monotonic types can be erased soundly. Syntactic criterion: a type is
possibly nonmonotonic iff it occurs as the type of a variable in a *positive
equality literal* `X ≈ t`. **Exhaustiveness axioms are precisely the
nonmonotonic formulas** — `∀x:two. x = a ∨ x = b` is the paradigm case that
must keep its guards. This is the sharpest published analysis of when
constructor-exhaustiveness may be stated bare in an untyped domain.

**Indexed families: do not exist in HOL** (cf. Gunter, *Why we can't have
SML-style datatype declarations in HOL*, TPHOLs 1992). The HOL substitute for
`le`, `even`, `reflect` is the **inductive predicate package**: `le` is a
boolean-valued lfp predicate whose intro rules and **elimination/cases rules —
i.e., inversion lemmas with argument equations**
(`even n ⟹ (n = 0 ⟹ P) ⟹ (⋀k. n = Suc (Suc k) ⟹ even k ⟹ P) ⟹ P`) are
ordinary lemmas passed to ATPs when selected. So HOL systems never face
type-level index unification: **indices live at the predicate level, and
"inversion with index equations" is shipped as plain lemmas**, made sound by
the type encoding around them.

## 2. Sledgehammer — SMT side (JAR 2013) and native (co)datatypes

**Citations.** Blanchette, Böhme, Paulson, *Extending Sledgehammer with SMT
Solvers*, JAR 51(1):109–128, 2013. Reynolds & Blanchette, *A Decision Procedure
for (Co)datatypes in SMT Solvers*, CADE-25 / JAR 2017.

**JAR 2013 translation.** Monomorphization (iterative instantiation of type
variables from the problem's ground types), λ-lifting, explicit `@` where
needed; HOL types map to SMT **uninterpreted sorts** (many-sorted target makes
guards unnecessary). **No datatype axioms are declared**; `declare-datatypes`
is not used; datatype properties arrive only as selected lemmas. Z3 proofs are
reconstructed/replayed, catching residual unsoundness.

**Native datatypes (JAR 2017 evaluation, §5).** Sledgehammer was extended to
emit SMT-LIB `declare-datatypes` for CVC4. Goals + 16/256 monomorphized lemmas;
"the solvers are trusted as oracles" (no reconstruction for datatype
inferences). Gains: +5% with 16 lemmas; 4 proofs needed acyclicity, 17 needed
the codatatype uniqueness rule.

## 3. SMT-LIB datatypes theory; CVC4/cvc5 and Z3

**Citations.** Barrett, Shikanian, Tinelli, JSAT 3:21–46, 2007. Reynolds &
Blanchette (above). cvc5 datatypes documentation. SMT-LIB 2.6+ spec.

**The theory, symbolically (Reynolds–Blanchette §2).** Discriminators
`d_ij(t) ≔ t ≈ C_ij(s¹_ij(t), …)`:

```
Distinctness:     C_ij(x̄) ≉ C_ij'(ȳ)                    if j ≠ j'
Injectivity:      C_ij(x₁,…,x_n) ≈ C_ij(y₁,…,y_n) → x_k ≈ y_k
Exhaustiveness:   d_i1(x) ∨ ⋯ ∨ d_im_i(x)
Selection:        s^k_ij(C_ij(x₁,…,x_n)) ≈ x_k
Induction:        (per-constructor IH schema)
```

"Exhaustiveness helps ensure that 'no junk' exists, whereas distinctness and
injectivity guarantee that 'no confusion' can arise." **Selectors on the wrong
constructor are left completely unspecified** (total but underspecified);
Barrett et al.'s default-value selectors yield spurious equalities like
`s(D) ≈ s(E)`. For **universal** problems induction can be replaced by the
**acyclicity schema** ("constructor terms cannot be equal to any of their
proper subterms"); codatatypes need the dual **uniqueness schema**. "These two
rules … cannot be finitely axiomatized, so they naturally belong in a decision
procedure." The calculus splits (`Split`) only when a selector is applied or
the type is finite — case analysis is *demand-driven*, not an upfront
disjunction.

**Indexed families: none.** Monomorphic many-sorted setting; SMT-LIB/cvc5
support *parametric* ADTs only (`par`). The closed-world semantics (initial
algebra of the constructors) is exactly what an indexed family breaks: in
`vector A n` the set of legal constructor terms at each index is constrained by
index equations, which the SMT datatypes theory cannot express — every
attempted use must either monomorphize indices away (Lean-auto's route, §6) or
move typing to a predicate (F\*'s route, §4).

## 4. F\* — the closest existing design for indexed inductives in a (nearly) untyped target

**Citations.** *Understanding how F\* uses Z3*, F\* book (uth_smt.html).
Aguirre, *Towards a Provably Correct Encoding from F\* to SMT*, MPRI report,
Inria Paris 2016. GitHub issues FStarLang/FStar#1542, #692.

**Term/type encoding.** Single uninterpreted sort `Term`; F\* types are
themselves `Term`s; typing is a binary fuel-instrumented predicate
`HasTypeFuel (Fuel Term Term) Bool`. Primitives boxed (`BoxInt : Int → Term`
with projection axioms); application by `ApplyTT`. **Essentially untyped FOL
with a typing predicate — structurally the same regime as CoqHammer's
target**, but with SMT `:pattern` triggers controlling instantiation.

**Refinement types are expanded shallowly, per definition, with an iff:**

```
∀x. HasType x nat  ⟺  HasType x int ∧ BoxInt_proj_0(x) ≥ 0   :pattern (HasType x nat)
```

Function definitions are type-guarded equations
`∀x̄. HasType(x₁,t₁) → ⋯ → f x̄ = E(e)`; quantifiers relativized. Aguirre proves
proof-theoretic soundness for a fragment; the key lemma is that **type guards
on equality axioms are what makes FOL proofs invertible back into well-typed
F\* derivations**.

**Datatype axioms (per inductive; `unat = Z | S unat`):**

```
Projection/injectivity:  ∀x. S_prec(S x) = x
Intro typing:            ∀f x. HasTypeFuel f x unat → HasTypeFuel f (S x) unat
Elim typing (fuel-decremented):
                         ∀f x. HasTypeFuel (SFuel f) (S x) unat → HasTypeFuel f x unat
Inversion (fuel-guarded exhaustiveness):
                         ∀f x. HasTypeFuel (SFuel f) x unat → is-Z x ∨ is-S x
```

The fuel decrement in elimination "prevents matching loops" between inversion
and elimination axioms. Recursive functions get separate `Fuel`
instrumentation (`factorial_fuel (SFuel f) x = …`, trigger-limited), plus
fuel-irrelevance and `factorial x = factorial_fuel MaxFuel x`; inversion depth
is bounded separately by `--ifuel`.

**Indexed families — the flagged item.** F\* is the one production system that
emits **inversion axioms with index equations**. Index constraints appear (a)
in constructor typing axioms as `HasType` premises and index expressions in
the conclusion's type instance:

```
∀a n x xs. HasType a Type ∧ HasType n nat ∧ HasType x a ∧ HasType xs (vec a (n-1))
           → HasType (Cons a n x xs) (vec a n)
```

and (b) in the per-type *fuel-guarded inversion* axiom
(`FStar.SMTEncoding.Encoding`'s `fuel_guarded_inversion`), where each disjunct
recovers constructor arguments via projectors **and equates the type's actual
indices with the constructor's imposed indices**. Because inversion is
pattern-triggered on `HasTypeFuel … x (T ā ī)`, it fires **per occurrence of a
typing assumption at a specific index instance** — F\* operationally realizes
"per-occurrence inversion with index equations" via E-matching rather than by
textual per-occurrence expansion. Closest analogue in the literature to full
indexed-family support in CoqHammer's translation.

**Soundness caveats.** (i) Issue #1542: the funext encoding was unsound
because F\*'s type-indexed `==` became Z3's native untyped `=`, "losing the
type information" — equality proven at one (refined) type leaked to another; a
proof of `False` resulted. **Untyped native equality + typed equality axioms
on an indexed `eq`-like family is a live minefield.** (ii) Issue #692:
partially applied indexed/refined inductives generated arity-wrong SMT
declarations (crash). (iii) Aguirre §7.4: removing type guards keeping only
`:pattern` filtering is an *unproven* optimization actually used in F\*.
(iv) Aguirre §6: monotonicity-based guard removal inapplicable — "inductive
types in F\* are not monotonous." (v) fuel/ifuel bounds trade incompleteness
against matching-loop divergence.

## 5. Dafny (and Verus) — ADT axiomatization in Boogie/Z3

**Citations.** Leino, *Dafny: An Automatic Program Verifier for Functional
Correctness*, LPAR-16, 2010, §1.9. Verus issue verus-lang/verus#1366.

All datatype values live in a single Boogie type `Datatype`. Five properties of
interest: (0) constructor injectivity, (1) distinctness, (2) exhaustiveness,
(3) partial order, (4) well-foundedness. **Dafny emits axioms for only three**:
(0) via destructors, (1) via a *category code* (`DatatypeCtorId`), (3) via an
integer `rank` (`∀a0,a1. rank(a0) < rank(Tree.Branch(a0,a1))`).
**(2) Exhaustiveness is deliberately NOT emitted**: "it can give rise to
enormously expensive disjunctions. Luckily, the property is usually not
needed, because the only case-split facility Dafny provides on datatypes is
the `match` expression and Dafny insists, through a simple syntactic check,
that all cases are covered." (4) is enforced by the type checker, not
axiomatized. Definitional axioms for `match`-bodied functions are emitted
**one per case** with constructor-shaped LHS triggers
(`∀u,v. LeafCount(Tree.Branch(u,v)) = LeafCount(u) + LeafCount(v)`) —
explicitly to avoid matching loops. No indexed types.

**Verus unsoundness case study (#1366).** Verus emitted for an *empty*
(field-less) struct the box/unbox axiom
`∀x:Poly. has_type(x, Empty) → x = Poly_Empty(unbox_Empty(x))`, which together
with datatype **height (rank) axioms** let Z3/Vampire derive `height_lt(h,h)`,
contradicting irreflexivity → proof of `False`. Lesson: rank/acyclicity axioms
+ carelessly total box/unbox coercions on degenerate types interact unsoundly —
the same risk shape arises for any `sig`/refinement unboxing plus a
structural-order axiom, and for uninhabited index instances.

## 6. Lean 4: Lean-auto, LeanHammer, lean-smt

**Citations.** Qian, Wu, Avigad et al., *Lean-auto: An Interface between Lean 4
and Automated Theorem Provers*, CAV 2025 (arXiv:2505.14929). Zhu, Clune,
Avigad et al., *Premise Selection for a Lean Hammer*, 2025 (arXiv:2506.07477).
Barbosa et al., *lean-smt*, CAV 2025 (arXiv:2505.15796).

**Lean-auto.** Pipeline: preprocessing (equational theorems for definitions) →
**monomorphization** (saturation-loop quantifier instantiation; λ→\* abstraction
of quasi-monomorphic terms into HOL variables; universe-level erasure) → HOL →
TPTP or SMT. **Inductive types**: "for an inductive type `T : ∀(α₁:U)…(αₙ:U).U`,
Lean-auto finds all relevant instances `T α₁…αₙ` and translates each instance
to a monomorphic inductive type in the SMT solver" — **indexed/parametric
occurrences are ground-instantiated and shipped as SMT `declare-datatypes`**;
for unsupported cases "users can always manually supply properties related to
inductive types as a workaround." No published treatment of genuinely
*indexed* families with non-ground indices; dependent arguments are "dynamic."
Reconstruction through Duper (kernel-checked); other ATPs trusted.

**LeanHammer** = neural premise selection + Aesop + Lean-auto translation;
inherits its inductive-type story.

**lean-smt.** Monomorphic FO fragment to SMT, cvc5 CPC proofs replayed.
Notable: **SMT-LIB sorts are nonempty, Lean types may be empty; reconstruction
fails any step depending on nonemptiness unless `Nonempty` is derivable** —
the dual of the exhaustiveness/cardinality pitfall (inhabitation instead of
upper bounds).

## 7. Agda → ATPs: agda2atp / Apia over FOTC

**Citations.** Bove, Dybjer, Sicard-Ramírez, *Combining Interactive and
Automatic Reasoning in First Order Theories of Functional Programs*,
FoSSaCS 2012.

Not a translation of Agda's type theory; a **first-order theory of
combinators (FOTC)** with one untyped domain `D`, equational conversion axioms
per program, **discrimination axioms** (`true ≢ false`, `0 ≢ succ d`,
injectivity of `succ`). **Totality/typing is a unary inductive predicate on
the untyped domain** — precisely CoqHammer-style guards:

```
data N : D → Set where
  zN : N zero
  sN : ∀ {n} → N n → N (succ n)      {-# ATP axiom zN sN #-}
```

Intro rules go to ATPs as axioms; the **induction schema is NOT sent** ("since
induction is a *schema* we cannot declare it as an axiom until it is
instantiated") — the user case-splits in Agda and each subgoal ships as
untyped TPTP. Coinductive predicates get a postfixed-point axiom that *is*
inversion-with-equations
(`∀xs. Stream xs → ∃x'∃xs'. Stream xs' ∧ xs ≡ x' ∷ xs'`) plus per-proof
gfp instances. No global exhaustiveness axioms; soundness via a Scott-domain
model; ATP output trusted.

## 8. Why3

**Citations.** Filliâtre & Paskevich, *Why3 — Where Programs Meet Provers*,
ESOP 2013. Paskevich, RR-7128, 2009. Why3 technical docs;
`src/transform/eliminate_algebraic.ml` (read from source).

Typed classical FOL with rank-1 polymorphism, ADTs, (co)inductive predicates;
per-prover drivers. **`eliminate_algebraic`**: constructors become abstract
function symbols; per-argument **projections** with axioms
`proj_i(C(ū)) = u_i`; an **inversion axiom in projection form (no
existentials)**:

```
∀u:τ.  u = C₁(proj₁¹(u),…) ∨ u = C₂(proj₂¹(u),…) ∨ …
```

(suppressible via `no_inversion` meta); pairwise discrimination or an integer
**indexer** `index(C(ū)) = k`; optional selector. No acyclicity, no induction
emitted — Why3 offers `induction_ty_lex` as a *goal* transformation instead
(induction handled meta-level). The projection-form inversion is sound only
because the target is *many-sorted*; dumped into untyped FOL it is exactly
Meng–Paulson's cardinality bomb unless guarded.

**`eliminate_inductive`** (doc, verbatim): "Replace inductive predicates by
(incomplete) axiomatic definitions, **construction axioms and an inversion
axiom**" — inversion-with-argument-equations at the predicate level; leastness
dropped (sound, incomplete).

## 9. Zenon Modulo / Dedukti translations of CIC

**Citations.** Boespflug & Burel, *CoqInE*, PxTP 2012. Burel et al., *Encoding
Proofs in Dedukti*, HaTT 2016. Delahaye et al., *Zenon Modulo*, LPAR 2013.
(HAL full texts bot-blocked; reconstructed from abstracts + tool docs.)

Dedukti = λΠ + rewrite rules. **`match` becomes an auxiliary case symbol with
one rewrite rule per constructor** (`case_T P b̄ (C ā x̄) ↪ b_C x̄`); **`fix`
unfolds only on constructor-headed recursive arguments** (the ι-guard — same
idea as F\*'s fuel and Dafny's constructor-shaped triggers, enforced by
rewriting). For **indexed families** the case symbol carries indices and the
return family; since rewriting happens only at constructor patterns, index
specialization per constructor is performed *by computation* — **no inversion
axioms exist at all; inversion = evaluation**. Lessons: (i) whatever the
prover can treat as *oriented computation* should not be an axiom; (ii)
guarded fix-unfolding is *the* universal divergence-prevention mechanism;
(iii) this route needs deduction-modulo prover support that mainstream ATPs
lack.

## 10. Liquid Haskell / refinement types

**Citations.** Vazou, Seidel, Jhala, Vytiniotis, Peyton-Jones, *Refinement
Types for Haskell*, ICFP 2014. Vazou et al., *Refinement Reflection*, POPL
2018 (arXiv:1711.03842).

**Shallow, per-constructor, axiom-free.** LH deliberately avoids datatype
axioms ("axioms render SMT solvers inefficient, and render the VC mechanism
unpredictable"). A **measure** (one structurally-recursive equation per
constructor) is lifted into the constructors' refined types:

```
Nil  :: {v:List a | llen v = 0}
Cons :: x:a -> l:List a -> {v:List a | llen v = 1 + llen l}
```

The SMT solver sees only quantifier-free VCs over uninterpreted functions: at
every **pattern-match site**, the checker adds the matched constructor's
refinement (with the actual index/measure equations for that branch) to the
environment — **per-occurrence inversion with measure equations, performed by
the type checker, never by the solver**. Exhaustiveness is a Haskell-side
totality check, never an SMT disjunction. *Refinement reflection* (POPL 2018)
reflects function definitions into their types with PLE
(proof-by-logical-evaluation) as a complete terminating unfolding strategy —
the SMT-side analogue of fuel, driven by the checker. The most consistent
published embodiment of "shallow, occurrence-driven expansion beats global
axioms"; directly corroborates the extraction branch's shallow `sig`
expansion. Trade-off: unbounded inversion depth needs an explicit inductive
lemma.

## 11. Cross-cutting soundness pitfalls (synthesis)

1. **Exhaustiveness in an untyped/open domain** is the canonical unsoundness:
   `X=a ∨ X=b` bounds the whole universe (Meng–Paulson §2.3). Sound options:
   (a) guard/tag it (LMCS 2016 — exhaustiveness axioms are exactly the
   nonmonotonic sentences); (b) don't emit it and case-split meta-level
   (Dafny, Why3 induction, LH, FOTC); (c) make it solver-internal and
   demand-driven (CVC4 `Split`).
2. **Native untyped equality vs. type-indexed equality**: F\* #1542 — equality
   established at a refined/indexed type escapes its type; proof of `False`.
3. **Selector/coercion totality on degenerate types**: default-value selectors
   create spurious equalities; Verus #1366 got `False` from box/unbox
   round-trips on an *empty* struct interacting with rank axioms. Uninhabited
   index instances (`vector A n` with contradictory indices, `Fin 0`) are the
   indexed-family version of this hazard.
4. **Inhabitation direction**: SMT sorts are nonempty; CIC types may be empty
   (lean-smt refuses nonemptiness-dependent steps; LMCS guards need explicit
   nonemptiness witnesses which are *wrong* for empty types).
5. **Matching loops**: universally mitigated by firing recursion-consuming
   axioms only on constructor-shaped or fuel-decorated triggers (Dafny
   per-case definitional axioms; F\* fuel with decrement in the *elimination*
   axiom; Dedukti guarded rewrite; PLE). Any per-type inversion axiom for
   indexed families must be guarded likewise or it self-instantiates through
   its own index-recovering disjuncts. Untyped ATP targets have no `:pattern`
   mechanism — a real design asymmetry vs. F\*.
6. **Acyclicity/induction are not finitely axiomatizable**: acyclicity schemas
   or rank functions suffice for universal goals; omitting them is sound but
   loses proofs (`x ≠ S x`); including rank axioms is where Verus got burned.
7. **Unproven pattern-based guard elision**: F\* removes some `HasType` guards
   relying on `:pattern` filtering alone (Aguirre §7.4: "no formal
   justification was ever given").

**Where "per-occurrence inversion with index equations" already appears:**
(i) **F\***: per-type, fuel-guarded, pattern-triggered inversion axioms whose
disjuncts carry constructor-argument projections and index equations; (ii)
**Isabelle inductive predicates**: `.cases` elimination lemmas with explicit
argument equations, passed as ordinary lemmas; (iii) **Why3
`eliminate_inductive`**: one inversion axiom per inductive predicate,
leastness dropped; (iv) **FOTC coinductive `gfp₁` axioms**:
inversion-with-equations in explicitly untyped FOL; (v) **Liquid Haskell**:
inversion performed by the checker at each match site, equations injected into
the local environment; (vi) **Dedukti/CoqInE**: inversion as computation via
constructor-pattern rewrite rules. Nobody except F\* does it for *type-level*
indexed families in a FOL/SMT target; Lean-auto ground-monomorphizes indices
away; the SMT datatypes theory excludes them.

**Minor related tools.** SMTCoq (CAV 2017): Coq→SMT for bool/Z/arrays
fragment, no inductives beyond enumerations. Sniper/Trakt (CPP 2023,
arXiv:2204.02643): generates, as *Coq-proved lemmas*,
injectivity/distinctness/projection/"generation" (exhaustiveness) statements
per parametric ADT before dispatch — each axiom certified; indexed families
out of scope. HOL(y)Hammer/MizAR: same regime as Sledgehammer.
