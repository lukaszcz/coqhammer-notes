\title{Program Extraction and Specification Extraction:\\
  A Sound Shallow Translation of Dependent Type Theory\\
  into First-Order Logic}
\author{}
\date{AI draft of \today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  Hammer-style automation for proof assistants based on the Calculus of
  Inductive Constructions (CIC) translates goals and premises into untyped
  first-order logic (FOL) and discharges the resulting problems with
  automated theorem provers.  We give a precise formulation of a translation
  architecture that factors this translation through the intermediate
  representation of Letouzey's program extraction: the translation of a
  definition $c \defeq t : \tau$ consists of (i)~\emph{program extraction} ---
  the erasure $\erase{t}$ of the body into an untyped functional language,
  compiled to \emph{per-constructor pattern equations} in FOL, and
  (ii)~\emph{specification extraction} --- a realizability reading of the
  type $\tau$ as a first-order formula about the FOL constant~$\Ihat{c}$.
  Both components are \emph{shallow}: propositions and specifications are
  mapped directly to first-order connectives and quantifiers computed at
  translation time, refinement-type payloads are expanded inline at every
  occurrence, and no inhabitation predicate mediates the logical structure.
  We define a monomorphic first-order fragment $\CICm$ of CIC with inductive
  datatypes, refinement types, inductive predicates, singleton eliminations
  and structural fixpoints, and prove the translation sound: from any
  well-formed definitional environment we construct a first-order model of
  all emitted axioms --- over the open term model of the erasure calculus,
  with explicit ill-typed ``junk'' elements --- in which the translation of
  the goal reflects a proof-irrelevant, classical truth semantics of the
  source.  Consequently the translation of a consistent environment is
  consistent, and no refutable goal becomes provable.  As a by-product we
  obtain a rigorous account of a known design question for such translations:
  the classical single \emph{guarded disjunctive} axiom for a pattern match
  is false at junk elements --- its type guard is mandatory --- whereas the
  per-constructor equations produced by extraction hold at \emph{all}
  elements of the model, so the guards on matched terms can be dropped, and
  the two forms are interderivable relative to the structural axioms of the
  datatype.
\end{abstract}
