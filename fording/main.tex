\title{Fording:\\
  A Sound Shallow Translation of Indexed Inductive Families\\
  into First-Order Logic}
\author{{\L}ukasz Czajka}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  Hammer-style proof automation for the Calculus of Inductive
  Constructions (CIC) translates goals and library facts into untyped
  classical first-order logic (FOL) and runs automated theorem provers
  on the result.  A companion paper carries this out for a monomorphic
  fragment with non-indexed inductive datatypes, factoring the
  translation through program extraction and proving every emitted
  axiom valid in one proof-irrelevant term model whose domain contains
  ill-typed ``junk''.  This paper extends the calculus, the
  translation and the soundness proof to \emph{indexed inductive
  families}: vectors, finite sets, \textsf{reflect}-style views,
  equality-like singletons.  The mechanism is McBride's \emph{fording}
  --- index constraints become equational payload, stated but never
  solved at translation time.  A family is represented by a
  $(1{+}m)$-ary guard predicate; its emitted theory is the first-order
  image of its ford: constructor typing with index conclusions, a
  guarded inversion axiom whose disjuncts carry constructor payloads
  and index equations, and unguarded per-constructor match equations.
  The model interprets a family in stages as a set of (index-tuple,
  element) pairs, with an \emph{index-reflection} lemma as the
  semantic content of fording; every emitted axiom holds in the model,
  so the translation is sound and the emitted theory consistent.  The
  model then arbitrates design questions documented in implementations
  of related encodings: omitting the inversion guard collapses the
  universe and is \emph{inconsistent} for every family once some
  two-constructor datatype is declared; forgetting the index arguments
  of guard predicates reproduces the \fstar{} \#1542 unsoundness;
  match equations need no guards even at statically impossible
  branches, index-premised variants being sound but strictly weaker;
  per-occurrence expansion of the ford is sound in both polarities and
  logically inert; index uniqueness is derivable while type-former
  injectivity is inexpressible by design; and the unconditional
  singleton collapse is exactly where the model's uniqueness of
  identity proofs is spent.
\end{abstract}
