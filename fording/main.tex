\title{Fording:\\
  A Sound Shallow Translation of Indexed Inductive Families\\
  into First-Order Logic}
\author{}
\date{AI draft of \today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  Hammer-style proof automation for the Calculus of Inductive Constructions
  (CIC) translates goals and premises into untyped first-order logic (FOL)
  and runs classical automated theorem provers on the result.  A companion
  paper defines such a translation, factored through program extraction, for
  a monomorphic fragment with non-indexed inductive datatypes, and proves it
  sound against a proof-irrelevant term model containing ill-typed ``junk''
  elements.  The present paper extends the architecture, and its soundness
  proof, to \emph{indexed inductive families} --- vectors, finite sets,
  bounded numbers, \textsf{reflect}-style views, and user-declared
  equality-like singletons.  The mechanism is McBride's \emph{fording}:
  every constructor $\wcns{c} : \forall \tup{a}.\ I\,\tup{t}(\tup{a})$ is
  treated as a constructor of a non-indexed type carrying the equational
  premises $\tup{u} \feq \tup{t}(\tup{a})$, so that index constraints become
  propositional payload, stated --- never solved --- at translation time.
  The translation remains \emph{shallow}: index constraints arrive at the
  prover as first-order equations inside guard formulas and inversion
  axioms, matches on families compile to per-constructor rewrite equations,
  and no inhabitation predicate mediates the logical structure.  We define
  the source calculus (a stratified fragment of CIC with indexed datatypes,
  indexed inductive predicates, forded dependent case analysis, and
  elimination of index-determined propositional singletons), the erasure to
  an untyped functional calculus, the emitted first-order theory, and a term
  model with junk in which every emitted axiom is proved valid; soundness
  and consistency of the translation follow.  The model construction doubles
  as an arbiter for the design questions documented in implementations of
  related translations: the guard of a family's inversion axiom is not
  merely necessary --- for families that specialise indices to
  constructor forms its omission is \emph{inconsistent}; forgetting the
  index arguments of guard predicates reproduces, and is refuted by, the
  type-indexed-equality unsoundness observed in \fstar{} (issue \#1542);
  per-constructor match equations need no guards even at statically
  impossible branches; index equations for forced arguments are redundant;
  per-occurrence inlining of the forded inversion disjunction is sound in
  both polarities and interderivable with the axiomatic form; and the
  unconditional collapse of singleton eliminations is exactly the point
  where the model's validation of uniqueness of identity proofs is spent.
  Every obstruction catalogued in the dependent-pattern-matching literature
  --- heterogeneous equality, Streicher's K, green slime --- is either
  validated by the model or dissolved by the untyped target.
\end{abstract}
