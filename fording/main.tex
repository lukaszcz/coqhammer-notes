\title{Fording:\\
  A Sound Shallow Translation of Indexed Inductive Families\\
  into First-Order Logic}
\author{}
\date{AI draft of \today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
  Hammer-style proof automation for the Calculus of Inductive
  Constructions translates goals and library facts into untyped
  classical first-order logic and runs automated theorem provers on
  the result.  A companion paper does this for a monomorphic fragment
  with non-indexed datatypes, factoring the translation through
  program extraction and proving every axiom valid in one
  proof-irrelevant term model whose domain contains ill-typed
  ``junk''.  This paper extends the calculus, translation and
  soundness proof to \emph{indexed inductive families} --- vectors,
  finite sets, \textsf{reflect}-style views, equality-like singletons
  --- by McBride's \emph{fording}: index constraints become equational
  payload, stated but never solved at translation time.  A family
  becomes a $(1{+}m)$-ary guard predicate; its emitted theory is the
  first-order image of its ford: constructor typing with index
  conclusions, guarded inversion carrying constructor payloads and
  index equations, and unguarded per-constructor match equations.  The
  model interprets a family in stages as a set of (index tuple,
  element) pairs; an \emph{index-reflection} lemma is the semantic
  content of fording; every emitted axiom holds, so the translation is
  sound and the emitted theory consistent.  The model arbitrates
  design questions documented for related encodings: omitting the
  inversion guard collapses the universe and is \emph{inconsistent}
  once any two-constructor datatype is declared; index-forgetting
  guards reproduce the \fstar{} \#1542 unsoundness; match equations
  need no guards even at statically impossible branches,
  index-premised variants being strictly weaker; per-occurrence
  expansion of the ford is sound in both polarities and logically
  inert; index uniqueness is derivable while type-former injectivity
  is inexpressible, by design; and the singleton collapse is exactly
  where the model spends uniqueness of identity proofs.
\end{abstract}
