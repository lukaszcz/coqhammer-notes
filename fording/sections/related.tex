\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Fording and the elimination of dependent pattern matching.}
The constraint technique is McBride's \cite[\S 3.5]{McBride1999},
including coalescence (our \cref{lem:n-one}) and the worked
$\mathsf{vtail}$ example that \cref{ex:vhead-vtail} replays inside
$\CICi$; the datatype-level formulation used in \cref{sec:fording} is
stated by Zhang \cite{Zhang2023}.  Goguen, McBride and McKinna
\cite{GoguenEtAl2006} eliminate Coquand-style dependent pattern matching
\cite{Coquand1992} into eliminators plus heterogeneous equality and K,
via specialization by unification; their five unification transitions
(deletion, solution, injectivity, conflict, cycle) are exactly the
first-order theory of constructors, and \cref{rem:slime} is the
observation --- made there for elaboration, here as a soundness theorem
--- that a saturation prover performs these transitions as ordinary
equational inference, with defined-function-headed indices handled
strictly better.  The without-K refinements of Cockx et al.\
\cite{CockxEtAl2014,CockxDevriese2018} supply the exact accounting of
which transitions require K/UIP; \cref{sec:formulas-uip} shows the model
validates the unrestricted set, and \cref{rem:hur} imports their
negative results (Counterexample~35; no type-former injectivity, after
Hur \cite{Hur2010}).  Coq's \texttt{inversion} tactic performs fording
at use sites \cite{CornesTerrasse1995}; Monin's small inversions
\cite{Monin2010} show the generated content is derivable with no
axioms, which is what makes the reconstruction obligations of our
inversion axioms benign; the Equations package \cite{SozeauMangin2019}
implements the whole stack inside Coq, with forced-argument analysis
(after Brady, McBride and McKinna \cite{BradyEtAl2004}) as the
UIP-avoidance mechanism that \cref{lem:n-one} repurposes as an
optimisation.

\paragraph{Erasure and proof irrelevance.}  The program half follows
Letouzey \cite{Letouzey2003,Letouzey2004,Letouzey2008}: sort-directed,
index-blind erasure, with stored indices kept; MetaCoq mechanises it
with the $\mathsf{Subsingleton}$ criterion for case erasure
\cite{SozeauEtAl2020}.  Our $\tscase{}{}{}{}$ is the stratified image of
CIC's singleton elimination \cite{Rocq}, extended from ``parameters put
aside'' to forced arguments; that this extension under proof irrelevance
is exactly UIP is the analysis of Gilbert, Cockx, Sozeau and Tabareau
\cite{GilbertEtAl2019}, whose corrected criterion (non-forced arguments
propositional, orthogonal index patterns, guardedness) delimits what can
be \emph{definitional} --- our model needs only what is \emph{true} in a
proof-irrelevant semantics, which is why orthogonality is not required
here (\cref{def:forced-singleton}).  Werner's guarded singleton
reduction \cite{Werner2008} is the syntactic ancestor of
\cref{prop:guarded-scase}; the proof-irrelevant model tradition
\cite{Werner1997,MiquelWerner2003,LeeWerner2011} is where our
truth-value semantics lives, and Lee--Werner's ``singleton with a
canonical element'' is the denotational form of $\prfc$, whose
algebraic form is Atkey's $!_0x = {!}_0y$ in quantitative type theory
\cite{Atkey2018} and whose operational form is Idris~2's
quantity-$0$ erasure \cite{Brady2021}.  Brady, McBride and McKinna's
forcing, detagging and collapsing \cite{BradyEtAl2004} delete at run
time what our translation asserts as equations; their warning about
collapsing in open contexts is discussed after
\cref{prop:guarded-scase}.

\paragraph{Hammers and type encodings.}  The immediate context is the
CoqHammer translation \cite{CzajkaKaliszyk2018,CzajkaKaliszyk2016}, its
proof-theoretically sound core \cite{Czajka2018}, and the companion
paper \cite{Extraction2026} whose architecture this paper extends;
\cref{sec:intro-impl} and \cref{rem:impl-sites} give the
correspondence.  For HOL hammers, the translation stack and its
model-with-junk soundness proofs are due to Meng and Paulson
\cite{MengPaulson2008} and Blanchette et al.\
\cite{BlanchetteEtAl2016}; their monotonicity analysis --- a type's
guards are erasable iff no positive equality literal bounds it ---
identifies exhaustiveness axioms as never-erasable, which
\cref{prop:unguarded-inconsistent} sharpens for families.  HOL systems
never face type-level index unification: inductive families arrive
pre-forded, either as predicates whose \texttt{.cases} rules are
inversion-with-equations shipped as ordinary lemmas, or as subset types
($\vect\,A\,n$ as $\{l \mid \mathsf{length}\ l = n\}$) --- fording is
the default in weaker logics, which is indirect evidence for its
suitability as a translation target.  Sledgehammer's SMT route
\cite{BlanchetteEtAl2013} and the SMT datatypes theory
\cite{BarrettEtAl2007,ReynoldsBlanchette2017} axiomatise closed-world
initial algebras --- exhaustiveness, selectors, demand-driven splitting
--- which is exactly what an indexed family is not; Lean-auto
\cite{QianEtAl2025} accordingly ground-monomorphises index instances
before shipping \texttt{declare-datatypes}.  \fstar{} \cite{SwamyEtAl2016} is the closest
production design: a single-sorted target with a fuel-instrumented
typing predicate and per-family inversion axioms carrying index
equations \cite{FStarBook,Aguirre2016}; its documented incidents ---
\#1542 \cite{FStar1542} and the partially-applied-inductive \#692 ---
and the Verus rank-axiom incident \cite{Verus1366} are analysed against
this paper's design in \cref{rem:1542,ex:fin-empty}.  Aguirre's
soundness analysis of the \fstar{} encoding \cite{Aguirre2016} proves a
fragment proof-theoretically and observes that HOL-style
monotonicity-based guard removal does not apply to indexed inductive
types --- consistent with our finding that family guards are the
load-bearing ones.  Liquid Haskell \cite{VazouEtAl2014} performs
per-occurrence inversion with measure equations in the \emph{checker},
keeping the solver quantifier-free --- the most consistent embodiment of
occurrence-driven expansion, at the price of checker-driven unfolding
depth; Dafny \cite{Leino2010} deliberately emits no exhaustiveness
axiom and per-case definitional axioms with constructor-shaped
triggers, matching the shape of \cref{def:compile}'s output; Why3's
\texttt{eliminate\_algebraic} emits projection-form inversion sound
only in a many-sorted target \cite{FilliatrePaskevich2013}; the
Dedukti/CoqInE route \cite{BoespflugBurel2012} turns inversion into
rewriting, with index specialization by computation.  The first-order
metatheory of term algebras --- completeness of
injectivity/distinctness/domain-closure/acyclicity, the
non-finite-axiomatisability of acyclicity, and the
$\Pi^1_1$-completeness with uninterpreted symbols --- is Kov\'acs,
Robillard and Voronkov \cite{KovacsEtAl2017}; the translation emits the
finitely axiomatisable guarded fragment and omits acyclicity
(\cref{sec:limitations-completeness}).

\paragraph{Semantics of inductive families.}  Indexed inductive
definitions in type theory are Dybjer's \cite{Dybjer1994}; the
stage-based interpretation of \cref{def:interp} is the classical
iterated-inductive-definition construction \cite{Aczel1977}, in the
indexed form used by set-theoretic models of CIC
\cite{Werner1997,LeeWerner2011}.  The rank-restricted treatment of
fixpoints is the semantic counterpart of guarded/sized recursion
\cite{Gimenez1995,BartheEtAl2004}, extended here with element ranks
made well defined by \cref{lem:index-reflection}(3).
