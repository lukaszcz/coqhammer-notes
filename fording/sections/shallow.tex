\section{Shallowness}
\label{sec:shallow}

Shallowness is the design constraint that shaped every clause of
\cref{sec:translation}: first-order ATPs are heavily optimised for the
primitives of first-order logic, so a translation is efficient to the
extent that the logical structure of the source arrives at the prover
\emph{as} first-order structure, with no inhabitation predicate and no
bridge axioms to instantiate \cite[\S 1.1]{Czajka2018}.  The companion
paper articulates this as a checklist S1--S5 and verifies it for the
non-indexed translation; we re-verify each item on the extended
translation and add the indexed-specific item S6.

\begin{description}[leftmargin=2em]
\item[S1 (Native logical structure).]  $\Ff$ maps every connective and
  quantifier of a shallow proposition to its first-order counterpart and
  source equality to $\feq$, at translation time (\cref{def:guard-F});
  nothing indexed changes this.  Expansion variants
  (\cref{def:expansion}) go further in the same direction: the
  \emph{content} of a family occurrence --- payload and index equations
  --- becomes part of the formula in which it occurs.

\item[S2 (No inhabitation predicate).]  $\SigFOL$ contains no binary
  has-type predicate and no term encoding of types
  (\cref{def:signature}).  The typing-flavoured atoms are the
  per-family guard predicates, now $(1{+}m)$-ary; their index argument
  places carry \emph{terms}, which is first-order structure the prover
  indexes and unifies natively.  This is the point where the present
  design parts company with the \fstar{} encoding --- to our
  knowledge, the one production system emitting inversion axioms with
  index equations for type-level families \cite{FStarBook,Aguirre2016}: there, types are terms and
  typing is a fuel-instrumented binary predicate, so inversion must be
  throttled by fuel and triggers to avoid matching loops between
  inversion and elimination axioms; here there is no self-instantiating
  typing axiom to loop on --- the inversion axiom is guarded by an atom
  that only constructor typing produces, and (in expansion variants)
  hypothesis-side content is inlined at translation time rather than
  recovered by E-matching at solve time.  The residual caveat is
  SMT-specific: a trigger discipline for the guarded inversion axioms
  of recursive families would be needed by SMT backends, which have no
  analogue of a saturation prover's fair clause selection.

\item[S3 (Per-occurrence payload expansion).]  Refinement payloads are
  expanded inline at every occurrence, in both polarities, as before.
  The forded view extends this uniformly to families:
  \cref{thm:expansion} licenses inlining constructor payloads
  \emph{and index equations} at any chosen occurrence, in both
  polarities, and \cref{prop:expansion-derivable} shows the choice is
  operational.  ``Index equations are just more propositional
  payload.''

\item[S4 (Proofs never become terms).]  Erasure drops proof arguments,
  premise proofs of constructors, and the index-equation binders of
  forded branches (\cref{def:erasure}); specification extraction keeps
  the term unapplied across propositional premises
  (\cref{def:spec}).  Proof subterms \emph{inside index terms} vanish
  too (\cref{rem:proof-indices}), so no proof material reaches the
  prover even through the new index argument places.

\item[S5 (Definitions as oriented computation).]  Pattern-matching
  definitions over families compile to unguarded, orientable,
  non-overlapping rewrite equations (\cref{rem:eq-shape},
  \cref{prop:match-equations}) --- for $\mathsf{vmap}_g$, literally the
  recursive program.  Case-analysis content (a search operation) is
  segregated into the guarded inversion axioms, emitted once per family;
  provers pay for splitting only when a proof needs it.

\item[S6 (Index constraints as equations, stated not solved).]  All
  index information of a family reaches the prover as first-order
  equations between ordinary terms --- in inversion disjuncts, in
  expanded guards, in constructor-typing conclusions --- computed at
  translation time by fording, never by unification.  Three classical
  obstructions dissolve at this interface, each by a theorem of this
  paper rather than by fiat: heterogeneous telescopic equations are
  stated pointwise in the untyped $\feq$ (\cref{def:theory}; the
  formation problem of \cite{McBride1999} does not exist in $\SigFOL$);
  defined functions in index positions are handled by superposition
  against their defining equations (\cref{rem:slime}), where
  elaboration-time unification is stuck \cite{McBride2014}; and the
  K/UIP side conditions of dependent pattern matching are validated by
  the model (\cref{sec:formulas-uip}).  Dually, the three soundness
  obligations the interface creates are discharged as theorems:
  guarded exhaustiveness only (\cref{prop:unguarded-inconsistent}),
  index-carrying guard atoms only (\cref{prop:index-dropping}), and
  no structural axioms beyond constructor images
  (\cref{rem:hur}).
\end{description}

\paragraph{The residue.}  As in the companion paper, partial
applications go through $\fapp$ and pointer constants, and anonymous
$\lambda$-abstractions, matches and fixpoints inside formulas are lifted
to fresh symbols --- unavoidable in a language without binders, and
local to terms.  The indexed extension adds one residue class:
\emph{induction on families} is not first-order and is not emitted
(\cref{sec:limitations-completeness}); the emitted content of a family
is exactly its ford --- constructor typing, guarded inversion,
injectivity, discrimination, and the defining equations of functions
over it.
