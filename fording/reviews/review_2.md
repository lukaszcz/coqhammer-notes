# Referee Report

**Manuscript:** *Fording: A Sound Shallow Translation of Indexed Inductive Families into First-Order Logic*

**Recommendation: Major revision — one blocking item.**

The revision is conscientious and, in one instance, better than what I asked for: my round-1 suggestion for delimiting the unguarded-inversion result (M5) was mathematically wrong, and the authors replaced it with a strictly stronger theorem (universe collapse from the family's own structural axioms) that I have verified in full and consider the most quotable single result of §8. Every round-1 item is addressed; I verified each disposition against the revised text (§1 below), re-derived the new mathematics (the strengthened Proposition 8.7 with its exact delimitation, the repair-family Remark 8.13, the non-overlap argument of Remark 5.6, the prefix-parameterised bootstrapping of §7), and checked the newly inlined proofs case by case.

Precisely because the previously by-reference proofs are now written out, a defect in the compilation layer that both the authors and I missed in round 1 has become visible — and it is not cosmetic. The value-translation clauses of Def. 5.2 and rule (3) of Def. 5.4 take lifted closures to be *all* free variables of the lifted term, including **fix-bound recursion variables** in the scope of a template map θ. For inputs that exercise this — conditional recursion on a computed test; a recursive closure passed to a higher-order function; both ordinary, well-typed, guarded, and inside $\mathcal{S}$ — the emitted axioms are false in $\mathfrak{M}$, and, through the hash-consing convention of Def. 5.1, two legal definitions can force an outright **inconsistent** emitted theory, contradicting Corollary 7.10 as stated. I give complete witnesses, the failing model computations, the blast radius (which is confined: every §8 result and every worked example in the paper survives untouched), and the repair directions, in §2. The repair is semantically forced and local to §5–§6, but it changes a soundness-critical definition and two lemmas, must be re-reviewed, and must be mirrored in the companion. Hence, regretfully, a second major-revision verdict — though a narrowly scoped one: I expect the next round to be quick, and nothing else stands between this paper and acceptance.

I owe the authors an apology contained in an explanation: the round-1 report claimed detailed verification of adequacy and compilation soundness. What I verified were the then-compact arguments, whose phrasing ("a complex head uses the interpretation of the lifted symbol via its witness") is *true for closed-under-θ heads* and silently undefined otherwise; the closure bookkeeping simply never surfaced. The revision's fully written-out Lemma 6.11 now contains the sentence "the closure parameters receiving the values ρ assigns them" (semantics.tex:417) — and ρ assigns nothing to a θ-bound variable, which is what exposed the gap. This is the self-containedness policy (M6) doing exactly the work it is supposed to do, and it should be counted in the revision's favour, not against it.

## 2. New major issue

### N1. Fix variables leak into lifted closures: emitted axioms false in $\mathfrak{M}$; with hash-consing, an inconsistent emitted theory (Def. 5.1, Def. 5.2, Def. 5.4)

**The gap.** The value translations of Def. 5.2 take the closure of a lifted complex head $h$ to be $\tup{w} = \mathrm{fv}(h)$ (translation.tex:109), and rule (3) of Def. 5.4 takes a case-lift's closure to be $\tup{w}' = \mathrm{fv}(\tup{br})$ (translation.tex:175–180). Nothing excludes **θ-bound recursion variables** from these free-variable sets. Since "source term variables double as first-order variables" (Def. 5.1, translation.tex:41) and $\forall.$ closes over "all free first-order variables" (translation.tex:165–166), a θ-bound $f \in \mathrm{fv}(h)$ ends up *universally quantified* in the emitted axioms, in the very argument place whose value the model-side witness dereferences. (On the alternative reading — θ-bound variables are not first-order variables — the emitted formulas are not sentences and compilation is not well defined; either way the development is broken on this input class.)

**Witness W1 (first-order; conditional recursion on a computed test).** Let $\mathrm{E}$ declare $\mathsf{nat}$, $\mathsf{bool}$, $A \defeq \mathsf{nat}$, $\mathrm{vect}$ (the running example), $\mathsf{iszero} \defeq \lambda m{:}\mathsf{nat}.\ \mathsf{case}(m;\ \{\mathsf{O} \Rightarrow \mathsf{true} \mid \mathsf{S}(p) \Rightarrow \mathsf{false}\})$, and

$$c \;\defeq\; \mathsf{fix}\,f{:}(\Pi n{:}\mathsf{nat}.\ \mathrm{vect}(n) \to \mathsf{nat}).\ \lambda n.\lambda v.\ \mathsf{case}\bigl(v;\ \{\mathsf{vnil} \Rightarrow \mathsf{O} \mid \mathsf{vcons}(k,a,w) \Rightarrow \mathsf{case}(\mathsf{iszero}\ k;\ \{\mathsf{true} \Rightarrow f\,k\,w \mid \mathsf{false} \Rightarrow a\})\}\bigr).$$

This is well typed by the fix rule (the body is a λ-prefix over a case on the guard argument $v$; branch obligations check), guarded ($\mathrm{grd}_2$: the sole occurrence of $f$ heads $f\,k\,w$ with $a_2 = w \in \tup{y}^{\mathrm{rec}}$), and every declaration type is shallow. It is, morally, "recurse if a computed test succeeds" — everyday code, not a corner case.

Compilation: the root yields $\theta = [f \mapsto (\hat{c}, (), 2)]$ and $\mathrm{B}_\theta(\hat{c}(n,v);\ \mathsf{case}(v;\dots))$; rule (2) refines $v$; in the vcons branch the scrutinee $\mathsf{iszero}\ k$ is not a variable of $L$, so rule (3) lifts $g = \mathrm{X}(\lambda z.\mathsf{case}(z; \{\mathsf{true} \Rightarrow f\,k\,w \mid \mathsf{false} \Rightarrow a\}))$ with closure $\tup{w}' = \mathrm{fv}(\tup{br}) = (f, k, w, a)$ — **the θ-bound $f$ included** — and the tree emits, among others (writing $\hat{\mathsf{t}}, \hat{\mathsf{f}}$ for the bool constructor images):

$$(\beta)\qquad \forall f\,k\,w\,a.\ \ g(\hat{\mathsf{t}}, f, k, w, a) \;\feq\; \hat{c}(k, w)$$

(rule (2) on $z$, then rule (5) with the θ-template translating the spine $f\,k\,w$). The witness of $g$ is $e_g = \lambda z.\mathsf{case}(z; \{\mathsf{true} \Rightarrow f\,k\,w \mid \mathsf{false} \Rightarrow a\})$ with closure places $(f,k,w,a)$, so by Def. 6.9, $g^{\mathfrak{M}}(d_z, d_f, d_k, d_w, d_a) = [\,e_g[\hat{e}_f/f, \hat{e}_k/k, \hat{e}_w/w, \hat{e}_a/a]\ \hat{e}_z\,]$.

**(β) is false in $\mathfrak{M}$.** Take $d_f := [\Box]$, $d_w := [\mathsf{vnil}]$, $d_k, d_a$ arbitrary. The left side denotes $[\mathsf{case}(\mathsf{true};\ \mathsf{true} \Rightarrow \Box\ \hat{e}_k\ \mathsf{vnil} \mid \dots)] = [\Box\ \hat{e}_k\ \mathsf{vnil}]$, a stuck junk class. The right side denotes $[\,c\ \hat{e}_k\ \mathsf{vnil}\,] = [\mathsf{O}]$ (one δ-, one μ-, two β-, one ι-step). These are distinct $\lred$-normal forms, distinct classes by Theorem 4.3. So **Theorem 7.8(2) is false for this well-formed environment**: the emitted theory contains an axiom that fails in $\mathfrak{M}$, and Theorems 7.9 and Corollaries 7.10–7.11 lose their proofs for every environment exercising this configuration. The main rule-(3) emission $\forall.\ \hat{c}(n, \widehat{\mathsf{vcons}}(k,a,w)) \feq g(\widehat{\mathsf{iszero}}(k), f, k, w, a)$ is false the same way.

**Witness W2 (higher-order; recursive closure under a λ-lift).** The Λ-clause has the same disease: $c' \defeq \mathsf{fix}\,f.\ \lambda n.\lambda v.\lambda u.\ \mathsf{case}(v;\ \{\mathsf{vnil} \Rightarrow \mathsf{O} \mid \mathsf{vcons}(k,a,w) \Rightarrow u\ (\lambda x{:}\mathsf{nat}.\ f\,k\,w\,u)\})$ with $u : (\mathsf{nat}\to\mathsf{nat})\to\mathsf{nat}$ (shallow; $\mathcal{S}$ excludes only predicate quantification, Def. 3.14). The argument $\lambda x.\ f\,k\,w\,u$ is a $j{=}0$ spine with complex head, so Def. 5.2 lifts it with closure $(f,k,w,u)$; the emitted $\forall f\,k\,w\,u\,x.\ f_1(f,k,w,u) \fapp x \feq \hat{c}'(k,w,u)$ fails in $\mathfrak{M}$ at junk $d_f$ exactly as above. "Pass the recursive call as a callback" is, again, ordinary code.

**N1b: hash-consing makes it an inconsistency, refuting Corollary 7.10 as stated.** Def. 5.1 posits injective naming functions "from λ□ terms *(up to renaming of free variables)*" (translation.tex:35–39). Renaming does not see θ: $\lambda x.\ f\,k\,w\,u$ (recursion variable $f$) and $\lambda x.\ y_1\,y_2\,y_3\,y_4$ (four ordinary variables) are identified, so a second definition $d' \defeq \lambda y_1{:}T.\lambda y_2{:}\mathsf{nat}.\lambda y_3{:}\mathrm{vect}(y_2).\lambda y_4.\ h_0\ (\lambda x.\ y_1\,y_2\,y_3\,y_4)$ (any constant $h_0 : (\mathsf{nat}\to\mathsf{nat})\to\mathsf{nat}$) receives the *same* symbol $f_1$, whose tree emits $\forall \tup{y}\,x.\ f_1(\tup{y}) \fapp x \feq y_1 \fapp y_2 \fapp y_3 \fapp y_4$ by the variable-head clause. Chaining with W2's equation gives $\forall.\ y_1 \fapp y_2 \fapp y_3 \fapp y_4 \feq \hat{c}'(y_2,y_3,y_4)$ for *all* $y_1$. Now add two three-argument constants $d_3 \defeq \lambda k\,w\,u.\ \mathsf{O}$ and $d_4 \defeq \lambda k\,w\,u.\ \mathsf{S}\,\mathsf{O}$: their pointer axioms and defining equations give $\hat{c}'(\tup{y}) \feq \hat{d_3}^0 \fapp y_2 \fapp y_3 \fapp y_4 \feq \hat{\mathsf{O}}$ and likewise $\feq \hat{\mathsf{S}}(\hat{\mathsf{O}})$, whence $\hat{\mathsf{O}} \feq \hat{\mathsf{S}}(\hat{\mathsf{O}})$, contradicting discrimination for $\mathsf{nat}$: **the emitted theory of a five-definition, well-formed, shallow environment proves $\bot$.** (If hash-consing is instead read as skipping the second compilation, $d'$'s symbol carries $c'$'s equations and validity in $\mathfrak{M}$ fails equally; both readings are fatal. The same collision arises between two θ-bound contexts with different templates — e.g. two structurally recursive definitions sharing the closure shape $\lambda x.\ f\,k\,w\,u$ but differing at $\mathsf{vnil}$ — so N1b is not an artifact of mixing θ-bound with ordinary variables; the root cause is that B's output depends on θ while the naming key does not.)

**Blast radius — importantly confined.** The semantic half of the paper is untouched: $\llbracket\cdot\rrbracket$ interprets a fix as its erasure class, with no lifting, so the fundamental lemma, index reflection, coincidence at the type/proposition level, and all of §7's structure are unaffected. Every §8 result stands: the witnesses there ($\mathsf{vhead}$, the reflect view, the XOR structure, $\mathrm{E}_{\mathsf{unit}}$) are non-recursive or θ-closure-free, and I re-checked that none of Props. 8.3–8.16 relies on the broken clauses. Every worked example in the paper compiles within the safe region. What is conditional on the repair: well-formedness of Def. 5.2/5.4 output on the full fragment, Lemma 6.11's complex-head bullets, Lemma 6.12's roots and rule (3), the compilation-axiom family of Theorem 7.8(2), and Theorems 7.9/7.10/7.11 in the generality currently claimed.

**Repair.** Semantically the fix is forced: the closure place of a θ-bound $g$ must be pinned to the *value* of $g$, not quantified. Two concrete routes, which may be mixed:

1. **Pointer-value pinning.** Define $\mathrm{val}_\theta(g) \defeq f_g^0(\tup{w}_g)$ for $\theta(g) = (f_g, \tup{w}_g, m)$ (for the root template, $\hat{c}^0$; the template should record the pointer symbol). At every lift, replace θ-bound members of $\mathrm{fv}(h)$ by $\mathrm{val}_\theta(g)$ — in the value, in the rule-(3) emission, *and in the recursion left-hand side* (a fresh universally quantified variable there is falsified by the witness semantics, by the same computation as above). Consequences to work through: Def. 6.10 (coherence) needs the additional clause $\llbracket \mathrm{val}_\theta(g)\rrbracket_\rho = [\sigma_\theta(g)]$ — I checked it holds at both establishment sites (root: $(\hat{c}^0)^{\mathfrak{M}} = [c]$; fix-lift: the closed witness instance); Lemmas 6.11/6.12's lift bullets adjust mechanically; Remark 5.6 must admit $f^0$-headed subterms at θ-closure places of left-hand sides (non-overlap survives — such subterms are never rule-(2)-refined and cannot unify with the $\fapp$-headed pointer left-hand sides — but *left-linearity* can fail when $\tup{w}_g$-variables repeat elsewhere in $L$, so the orthogonality statement needs care).
2. **Definiens substitution at the root.** For the root template, $\sigma_\theta(g_0)$ is the λ□ *constant* $c$; substituting $c$ for $f$ in the lifted term before naming and lifting makes the closure ordinary, the witness closed, the key θ-free (dissolving N1b for root templates), and the body's recursive spines compile through the constant-head clause to exactly the template translation. For *inner* fix-lifts there is no constant, and substituting the fix term itself grows the input (the termination measure of Lemma 5.5 would need re-doing), so route 1 is the robust choice there.

In both routes the **naming functions must key on the θ-resolved term** (equivalently on the pair $(h, \theta{\restriction}\mathrm{fv}(h))$): injective naming on raw terms up to free renaming is exactly what N1b exploits. I would also suggest adding, as a sanity invariant, a one-line lemma that every emitted axiom is a sentence over ordinary first-order variables — the invariant whose absence let this slip — and a sibling of Remark 5.3 recording that the θ-closure configuration is reachable (W1) so that no future revision is tempted to argue unreachability.

**The companion paper must be fixed identically** — these clauses are inherited, as the authors' own M1 note anticipates for the sibling gap. I would also gently note that the *implemented* translation is probably unaffected (practical λ-lifters treat recursion variables specially as a matter of course); this is a defect of the formal development, not evidence of a production bug — but the formal development is precisely what this paper sells, and its round-1 M1 repair explicitly chose "totality is free" over unreachability arguments. The same standard applies here, with more force: this configuration *is* reachable.
